About Me

My name is Walter Nam and I'm pursuing a Bachelor's in Computer Science at Case Western Reserve University. Throughout my university career, I faced the challenge of solving unique problems with efficient algorithms and appropriate data structures in Java. My curiosity drove me to develop my own personal projects outside of class, including a Python Tetris game and a weather forecasting web application. In addition, I have been tearing through articles, books, and video tutorials on anything related to software development. I am a passionate learner of new PLs and frameworks and after picking up C++, I participated in the 2020 annual HACKCWRU hackathon, during which I built with my teammates a Virtual Synthesizer using the JUCE framework. I trained and tested this CNN using the ML library keras-tensorflow. As an undergraduate, I am interested at this time in pursuing a career in either software development or artificial intelligence. My passion drives me, and I look forward to solving more problems and learning new concepts.
